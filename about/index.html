About

Built for the checkride. Named for the standard.

"Clear cut" is how an answer should sound in the oral. It's how a lesson plan should read. It's how your preparation should feel the night before your ride. That's the bar for everything we make.

The Problem

CFI prep is drowning in material

There has never been more CFI study content available — and somehow the initial CFI checkride still carries one of the lowest first-attempt pass rates of any FAA practical test. The problem usually isn't a lack of information. It's that candidates spend months collecting material and almost no time learning to deliver it.

Clear Cut CFI takes the opposite approach: a structured path through the Flight Instructor ACS, taught in plain English, with practice quizzes that show you exactly where you're weak — so you spend your time teaching, not hoarding PDFs.

The Method

Three rules behind every lesson

Follow the ACS. If the standard doesn't ask for it, it doesn't pad the course. If it does, we hit it directly.

Teach the teaching. Knowing aerodynamics and teaching aerodynamics are different skills. Every concept comes with the "how would you put this on a whiteboard" angle.

Test honestly. Randomized question banks mean you can't memorize your way to a false sense of readiness.
